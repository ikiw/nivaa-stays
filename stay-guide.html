Local Guide

Your Pondicherry Companion

A handpicked guide to the best of Pondicherry — where to eat, what to see, how to get around. From the French Quarter to Auroville, the bits we'd recommend to a friend.

01

Eat

Café des Arts

Charming French Quarter café with crepes, salads and great coffee. Sit in the courtyard.

~10 min · French Quarter

Surguru

Local favourite for South Indian thali, dosas and tiffin. Vegetarian.

~7 min · Mission Street

Villa Shanti

Refined Indo-French dining inside a heritage villa. Perfect for an evening out.

~10 min · Suffren Street

Baker Street

French bakery with quiches, croissants, and the best lemon tart in town.

~9 min · Bussy Street

Kasha Ki Aasha

Rooftop café and craft store run by women artisans. Perfect for a quiet lunch.

~10 min · Rue Surcouf

Hotel Atithi (biryani)

If you want a proper Pondicherry biryani feast — non-veg, generous portions.

~12 min · Nehru Street
02

See

Promenade Beach (Rock Beach)

Sunrise walks here are unbeatable. Closed to traffic 6 PM – 7:30 AM. Gandhi statue, French War Memorial, cafés along the strip.

~12 min by car

French Quarter (White Town)

Mustard yellow walls, bougainvillea, quiet streets. Best explored on foot or our Vespa. Don't miss Rue Romain Rolland and Sri Aurobindo Ashram.

~10 min by car

Matrimandir & Auroville

The golden geodesic at the heart of Auroville. Book tickets a day in advance for inner chamber access. Allow half a day.

~25 min by car · book ahead

Paradise Beach

Quiet, clean stretch of sand reached by ferry from Chunnambar Boat House. Pack water + sunscreen.

~25 min · ferry needed

Sri Aurobindo Ashram

Spiritual heart of Pondicherry. Quiet, contemplative — open to visitors mornings and evenings.

~10 min · French Quarter

Botanical Garden

Colonial-era garden, good for a slow morning walk. Free entry.

~12 min

Ride

Take the Vespa.

Pondicherry is meant to be explored on two wheels — the streets are flat, distances are short, and parking is impossible in the French Quarter anyway. Our yellow Vespa is on-demand for guests.

04

Getting Around

Auto-rickshaws

Plentiful but always negotiate before getting in. Short hops in town: ₹80–150. To Auroville: ₹400–600. Ola/Uber are limited; autos are easier.

To/from Chennai Airport (MAA)

~3 hr drive (160 km). We can arrange a cab — ~₹3,000–3,500 one way. Pondicherry has its own small airport (PNY) with limited flights.

To/from Villupuram station

Nearest major rail hub, ~40 min away. Cab ~₹800. Frequent buses too.

Within Pondicherry

White Town is walkable. For Auroville and Paradise Beach, take the Vespa or a cab. Avoid driving in the French Quarter — parking is rough.

05

Local Tips

Best time to visit Matrimandir

Book the inner chamber visit a day ahead via auroville.org. Early morning slots are calmer.

Weather

Oct–Mar is pleasant. Apr–Jun is hot and humid. Jul–Sep brings monsoon — beautiful but wet.

Liquor

Pondicherry is a Union Territory with cheaper liquor than Tamil Nadu — many wine shops; bars in White Town.

Etiquette

Cover shoulders/knees inside the Ashram and Matrimandir. Photography restricted in many spiritual sites.
